USA vs China: A New World Order?

For over a century, the USA has been the dominant world superpower. This project aims to analyse the extent to which China is challenging that title.

Wealth of a Nation

I begin by analysing the wealth of the Chinese and USA populations. I look at the Mean and Median Wealth, and analyse each nations share of high net worth individuals. This data was sourced from the Credit Suisse Global Wealth Report 2021. This is a PDF, so I used Excel's 'Data from Picture' Function to change screenshots of the report into CSV files.

These maps show the USA is well ahead of China in Mean and Median wealth of their population. However, inequality is higher in the USA, as the mean wealth has a much higher difference to the median when compared to China. This is made clearer later where we see China's Gini Index is lower than the US.

Looking Forward: GDP Growth

I accessed the OECD API and used BeautifulSoup to scrape the data on long-term forecasted real GDP for China and the USA. This data was then stored in a Pandas dataframe, allowing me to manipulate and analyze the data efficiently. I used the Pandas library to convert the data into a JSON object, as well as a CSV file for visualization. This can be seen in my Google Colab file. The use of API means data will be refreshed automatically when the code is run, resulting in automation of data.

It is a fact that China has surpassed the United States in GDP and is anticipated to continue widening the gap in the future. This alteration in the economic power dynamic may have substantial implications for the balance of power.

Population and Social Characteristics

I display population pyramids for China and the US. The data was downloaded in csv format from a link on this website which they originally sourced from the UN.

The age structure of a nations population is informative about the future. Both indicate a structure where the middle-aged population make up a larger segment of the population whilst the younger cohorts have reduced in size. In the future, both will have a largely dependent population resulting in huge social welfare costs. The problem is greater in China but still problematic in the US. China also has an incredibly unbalanced gender dynamic, with more males in every bracket below 65.

Military Might

The military is a crucial aspect of a nation's ability to project hard-power globally. Lack of structured databases made compiling data on respective militaries difficult.

Below is a bubble diagram comparing gross military spending, military spending as a % of GDP and military personnel sizes between 2000-2019. I downloaded data into CSV format from Stockholm International Peace Research Institute to create this chart.

We see rises in gross spending for both China and US, a slight fall in Chinas spending as a % of GDP and a rise then fall for the US. The US spends significantly more on its military, whilst China has a much larger personnel base throughout the period.
